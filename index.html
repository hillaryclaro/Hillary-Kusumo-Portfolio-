ANNOTATION PROJECT

In this project, I worked on annotating rodeo event datasets including images and videos. I applied bounding boxes and keypoints to label riders, horses, bulls, and barrels. My goal was to ensure high-quality annotations for machine learning models, focusing on accuracy and consistency.

I carefully tracked objects across frames, handled occlusions, and separated each subject for precise labeling. Each frame was annotated at 30fps to maintain temporal consistency. Attention to detail was critical to prevent mislabeling and maintain dataset reliability.

This work sharpened my ability to manage complex datasets, handle repetitive tasks efficiently, and follow structured guidelines. Annotating real-world videos also enhanced my understanding of movement dynamics and spatial relationships, which are crucial for autonomous AI systems.

KEY GUIDELINES

To maintain consistency, I applied strict labeling rules. Each rider and horse were labeled separately. Tight bounding boxes ensured precise localization. Objects were tracked across frames for continuity, and occlusions were carefully handled to avoid annotation errors.

  • Label at 30fps
  • Separate rider and horse
  • Apply tight bounding boxes
  • Track same object across frames
  • Handle occlusion correctly
