ANNOTATION PROJECT

In this project, I annotated rodeo event datasets including images and videos using bounding boxes and keypoints. The goal was high-quality annotations for machine learning models.

I tracked objects across frames, handled occlusions, and separated each subject. Each frame was annotated at 30fps for temporal consistency.

This improved my ability to manage complex datasets, handle repetitive tasks efficiently, and understand movement dynamics and spatial relationships.

KEY GUIDELINES

Applied strict labeling rules to maintain consistency. Each rider and horse were labeled separately. Tight bounding boxes ensured precision. Objects tracked across frames for continuity, occlusions handled carefully.

  • Label at 30fps
  • Separate rider and horse
  • Apply tight bounding boxes
  • Track same object across frames
  • Handle occlusion correctly

Images demonstrate sample annotations with precise bounding boxes and keypoints for multiple subjects within each frame.

VIDEO EVALUATION

Evaluated multiple video samples to assess motion quality, audio synchronization, and visual realism using a structured rubric.

EVALUATION CRITERIA

  • Motion smoothness
  • Frame consistency
  • Audio sync
  • Visual realism

RUBRIC

  • 5: Perfect alignment and realism
  • 4: Minor issues
  • 3: Noticeable inconsistencies
  • 2: Major defects
  • 1: Poor quality

RESULT

Video 1 scored higher across all metrics. Motion was smoother, audio aligned, and visual fidelity was better. Video 2 had minor inconsistencies affecting the score. Final report highlighted differences.

Final Decision: Video 1 selected due to better motion quality and synchronization.
